Le premier et le dernier vers du premier tercet finissent à tour de rôle les tercets suivants. An old form, of a charming grace and naiveté, which many modern poets have tried to make fashionable.

One example will be worth more than all explanations; here is the masterpiece of villanelles: [full text of "J'ay perdu ma Tourterelle"].

One will notice that the villanelle is a kind of terza rima constructed with the same rhymes laid end to end. The first and the last lines of the first tercet complete the following tercets in turn. But one must stop on a tercet finishing with the first line ("I have lost my turtledove"), because the last line ("I want to go after her") is destined to tie the knot.
