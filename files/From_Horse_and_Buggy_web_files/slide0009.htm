No two of the eighteen are identical in rhyme scheme, length, and syllable count. They do not resemble each other but, in most cases, each resembles an actual musical villanella or villancico. It cannot possibly be said that there was anything resembling a poetic ‘form,’ let alone a fixed poetic form, for the villanelle in the sixteenth century.

Julie Kane. "How the Villanelle's Form Got Fixed." Diss., Louisiana State University, 1999.
